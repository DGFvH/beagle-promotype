# Beagle marketing hero — copy draft

Captured by `beagle-hero-visuals`. No reference material existed in this folder at
time of writing (only README.md + .gitkeep), so this copy is derived from the
brand tokens and the product truth in `BEAGLE_MVP.md` Section 1. Keep claims
truthful: Beagle is **human-approved** (you approve before anything goes live),
results come from **real analytics**, and Claude **proposes** within your design
system + guardrails — do not overclaim full autonomy.

## Shipped version

- **Eyebrow:** beagle
- **Headline:** Let Claude turn your hero section into a self-improving A/B test.
- **Subhead:** Connect your real page and analytics. Claude proposes a hero
  variant and a hypothesis that respect your design system and guardrails — you
  approve it, Beagle ships it with cookie-based assignment, then reads the real
  results back and breaks them down per segment.
- **CTAs:** Primary `See it work` (onStart) · `Connect your site` (onConfigure) ·
  `How it works` (onMethodology)
- **Loop strip:** Test · Measure · Decide · Evolve (unchanged — it is the real loop)

## Value-prop pillars (used in the trust row under the CTAs)

1. Claude-proposed variant + hypothesis
2. Respects your design system & guardrails (legal / style / do-not-change)
3. Real GA4 analytics, read back from live traffic
4. Per-segment heterogeneity (traffic source, device, …)
5. Human-approved — nothing goes live without your sign-off

## Alternatives considered (headline)

- "Evidence-based hero optimization, approved by you, run by Claude."
- "Your hero section, continuously tested against real traffic."
- "Stop guessing at your hero. Test it against real users — with Claude proposing
  every variant."
- "A self-improving A/B test loop for your hero section." (chosen variant is a
  warmer, agent-forward rephrasing of this)

## Why this framing

The previous hero ("Autonomous UI experiments that evolve in front of the room" /
"walk through eight completed generations… finish generation 9 live") was
pitch-demo framing. It described the *demo artifact*, not the product. The new
copy leads with the customer outcome (their hero, their analytics, their
approval) and names Claude + guardrails explicitly, which is the real
differentiator and stays inside the truthful-claims bar (Section 6).
