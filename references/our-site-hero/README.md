# references/our-site-hero — OUR Beagle marketing site hero

## Scope: THIS IS OUR OWN PRODUCT'S MARKETING SITE.
> This folder is about the hero section of **Beagle's own landing page** — the page that sells Beagle to prospective customers.
>
> ⚠️ **This is NOT the client site.** Do not put examples of the customer-hero-being-optimized here. Those belong in `references/client-hero-design-space/`. If you are unsure: *our-site-hero* = the hero a visitor sees when they land on beagle's website to learn what Beagle is; *client-hero-design-space* = the hero variants Beagle generates and A/B tests on a customer's website.

## Consumed by
- **Agent:** `beagle-hero-visuals` (primary)
- Cross-read by `beagle-lead` when reconciling our brand against the in-app `design-system`.

## FR IDs this informs
None of the FR-* requirements directly (the FRs are about the product engine, not our marketing site). This folder informs **product/marketing presentation only** — the visual first impression of Beagle itself. Keep it clearly separated from the FR-driven folders so an agent never confuses our marketing hero with a client variant (FR-A2 / FR-C1).

## What decision this informs
- The look, copy tone, layout, and visual language of Beagle's own marketing hero.
- When `beagle-hero-visuals` has to make a hard call on our hero (composition, headline voice, imagery, motion), it consults the inspiration here instead of guessing.

## What to drop here
- **Screenshots** of marketing hero sections we admire (SaaS / dev-tools / analytics landing pages). Name them descriptively, e.g. `inspo-linear-hero.png`, `inspo-vercel-hero.png`.
- **Links** to live marketing pages — add them to `LINKS.md` (one per line, with a one-line note on *why* it's here).
- **Zips** of any downloaded reference page / component (`*.zip`) dropped directly in this folder.
- Any draft copy, taglines, or value-prop wording for our hero (`our-hero-copy-draft.md`).

## Conventions
- Links → add to `LINKS.md` in this folder.
- Code / page snapshots → drop the `.zip` directly here.
- Always annotate *why* a reference is here (what specifically to learn from it), not just the URL.
